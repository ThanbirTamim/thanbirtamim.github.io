About Me

I am a software professional with a strong foundation in research and real-world system development, currently serving as a Senior Software Programmer in the Bangladesh Army. My work focuses on building advanced 2D and 3D GIS-based simulation systems used in military training and strategic operations. These systems blend geospatial data, real-time rendering, and procedural generation to create immersive, mission-critical environments. Previously, I worked as a Research Programmer at the Cyber Security Center of Daffodil International University, where I was involved in multiple data security projects and led cybersecurity training sessions for students and professionals. My core areas of interest include data security, procedural content generation, GIS in gaming and simulation, and the application of machine learning in spatial analysis. I am deeply passionate about education and research and aspire to pursue a long-term academic career. As I apply for a Lecturer position, I bring with me not only technical expertise and applied experience but also a strong commitment to mentoring students, fostering critical thinking and contributing to the advancement of knowledge through research and collaboration.
